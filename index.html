Timetastic

A time management app

This is a group project for UX/UI bootcamp that I attended on 2021/2022. In this project I took part in the wireframe, mockup and prototype phases.

Timetastic a time management app that helps users keep track of their time, form new habits and find motivation to plan their time. Inspired by the most common issue people are facing today and that is: I donâ€™t have the time for...And even though there are plenty of time management tools out there, people are still facing the same issue. While rapid transformation of technology allows us to have access to a variety of tools, information, and entertainment instantly, that same technology often creates cognitive overload and distraction. How can we find the balance and perhaps use that same technology to help us declutter our minds and find the motivation to pursue our passions while still completing all our daily tasks?

View Prototype

"Time management is one of my flaws and so I never prioritize managing my time, which in turn, makes me be behind for things. It's a vicious cycle." Quote from a User Interviewee

So we got many insightful results from our interviews and survey. To make sense of them, we then created an affinity diagram. The categories of our affinity diagram were tools, demographics, occupation, hobbies, pain points, wants, and current time management practices.

Our findings led to the creation of our user persona. Meet Miigwan Aki. As a 35-year-old mother of two children who often spends long hours working as a product designer, she is very busy. Miigwan does try to manage her time by planning her schedule in advance. However, she rarely sticks to her planned schedule and ends up feeling exhausted and with no free time. Miigwan needs motivation to stick to her planned schedule so that she can have more time to enjoy her favorite hobbies and interests including baking, dancing, quilting, camping, exercising, and hiking. Our app needs to help her stay committed to completing her tasks on time.

Problem Statemen

We have found that busy working parents that struggle with finding the right platform to keep track of their responsibilities and help them organize their time. As a result they aren't able to enjoy their hobbies and follow their passions.

Iteration

Using figma to create the new design of Timetastic.

Through the process of working on the prototypes we made many different design choices and even changed the layout of many of the screens to keep improving our app. Even though this is the final prototype we still have a lot we would want to improve on by adding more detailed features to help personalize the app.
